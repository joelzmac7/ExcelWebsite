ICU Registered Nurse

Travel Hot Job
📍 San Francisco, California
🏥 Memorial Hospital
📅 Posted: August 20, 2025

Job Overview

Assignment Length 13 Weeks
Start Date ASAP
Shift Night Shift (7PM-7AM)
Schedule 3x12 (36 hours/week)
Facility Type Level I Trauma Center
Bed Count 450 Beds

ICU Registered Nurse needed for a 13-week travel assignment in San Francisco, CA. The ideal candidate will have at least 2 years of ICU experience and active CA license. This position is at a prestigious Level I Trauma Center with state-of-the-art facilities.

As an ICU nurse at Memorial Hospital, you will be responsible for providing direct patient care to critically ill patients, monitoring vital signs, administering medications, and collaborating with the interdisciplinary team to ensure optimal patient outcomes.

This is an excellent opportunity to gain experience in a high-acuity setting while enjoying all that San Francisco has to offer. Housing stipend and travel reimbursement included in the compensation package.

Requirements

  • Active California RN license or compact license with ability to obtain CA license
  • Minimum of 2 years of recent ICU experience
  • BLS and ACLS certifications required
  • PALS and TNCC certifications preferred
  • BSN preferred
  • Experience with ventilator management and critical care protocols
  • Ability to work in fast-paced environment and handle high-acuity patients
  • Strong communication and teamwork skills

Benefits

  • Competitive weekly pay: $2,800 - $3,200
  • Housing stipend: $1,050/week
  • Travel reimbursement up to $500
  • Medical, dental, and vision insurance
  • 401(k) retirement plan
  • 24/7 clinical support
  • CEU reimbursement
  • Referral bonuses
  • License reimbursement

About Memorial Hospital

Memorial Hospital is a 450-bed Level I Trauma Center located in the heart of San Francisco. The facility is known for its cutting-edge technology, evidence-based practices, and commitment to patient-centered care. The ICU department consists of 24 beds with a 2:1 patient-to-nurse ratio and features the latest monitoring and life support equipment.

The hospital has received numerous awards for excellence in nursing care, including Magnet Recognition from the American Nurses Credentialing Center. Staff enjoy a collaborative work environment with opportunities for professional growth and development.
